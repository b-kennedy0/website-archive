In August 2021, I’m planning to go on a cruise with Ryan, Sara and Jess. It is an Alaskan Cruise departing from Seattle US and visiting parts of Alaska and Canada. The main part of the holiday is sight-seeing. Glaciers, Whales and everything Alaska has to offer.

In order to have the best possible time, I'm trying to get some financial support. I would be eternally grateful if you could support my holiday in any way you can.

For Chritsmas or my birthday, if you usually get me a gift or a card, perhaps this year you could consider giving towards one of my planned excursions instead? Whatever you can help with, even just the £1 you might have spent on a card would be amazing to chip away at cost!

We're planning the following excursions. If you decide to, you can select which of these you would like to support.

Sea Otter, Raptor Tour, Bear Sanctuary This is three unique wildlife experiences in Sitka. A cruise on the award-winning Sea Otter and Wildlife Quest, followed by a guided tour through the renowned Alaska Raptor Centre and finish with a visit to a sanctuary for orphaned Alaska Brown Bears.
Glacier & Whales This excursion is a nature trip where the onboard naturalist will explain the behavior and habitat of wildlife that we encounter, including humpback and killer whales, Steller sea lions, Dall's porpoise, harbor seals, bald eagles, bears and deer.
Gold Panning, Dogs sled tour, -40 Experience This excursion is first in the Klondike Gold Fields where we'll start with some gold panning, followed by a sled dog demonstration and finish with a chilly -40 degree experience.
Vancouver Whistler Sight-seeing - Located in the spectacular Coast Mountains of British Columbia just two hours north of Vancouver, Whistler is Canada’s favourite year-round destination. There are two majestic mountains with a vibrant base village.
Vancouver Grouse Mountain Sight-seeing - Rising 1,250 metres (4,100 feet) above Vancouver and just 15 minutes from the city’s downtown core, is the vast alpine playground of Grouse Mountain.  The iconic Red Skyride gives visitors and locals alike the opportunity to see the jaw-dropping majestic nature of B.C. unfold in front of them during the 8 minute ride from the Valley Station to the summit.
Seattle Space Needle Located at Seattle Center, the Space Needle stands at 605’ tall and is one of the most photographed structures in the world. The tower’s 520’ saucer-shaped “top house” offers visitors Seattle’s only 360-degree indoor and outdoor panoramic views of downtown, Mount Rainier, Puget Sound, and the Cascades and Olympic mountain ranges.
